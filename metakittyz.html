Step 1 of 8 — Saturday
Define Your Brand Identity
⏱ 30 minutes
What This Does

Everything your AI team creates comes from your brand identity. Get this wrong and your agents will produce generic content. Get it right and every piece they make sounds unmistakably like you.

How To Do It

Open a new document (Google Docs, Notion, or Notes app) and answer these questions honestly:

  • Brand name: What do you want to be known as?
  • Niche: What specific topic do you own? (Narrow wins.)
  • Target audience: Who exactly are you talking to? Age, struggles, dreams.
  • Brand voice: 3 adjectives that describe how you sound. (e.g., "Bold. Honest. Real.")
  • Your unfair advantage: What do you know or have that others don't?
  • Your big promise: In one sentence, what will you help people achieve?
💡 Pro Tip

Save these answers in a doc called "BRAND MASTER FILE." You'll paste this into every agent prompt this weekend. It's the most important document in your whole system.

Expected Output

A completed brand identity doc with all 6 answers. Don't overthink it — you can evolve this later. Done beats perfect today.

🎉

Brand identity locked in! You officially know who you are. Step 1 complete.

AI Agent
A smart AI assistant you give a specific job to. Like hiring a specialist — NOVA is your content specialist, GUARDIAN is your quality control specialist.
System Prompt
The instructions you give your AI before it starts working. It's how you tell the AI who it is, what it does, and how it should behave — every single time.
LLM (Large Language Model)
The brain inside tools like Claude and ChatGPT. An LLM is trained on massive amounts of text and can read, write, and reason like a human — but much faster.
Claude
An AI assistant made by Anthropic. This is your main tool for building and running all 6 agents. Think of it as your AI headquarters.
ChatGPT
An AI assistant made by OpenAI. Similar to Claude — great for content generation, brainstorming, and writing. Use both for different strengths.
Prompt
What you type into an AI to tell it what to do. The better your prompt (more specific, more context), the better the output you get back.
Token
How AI measures text — roughly 1 token = 1 word. AI tools have token limits (how much text they can process at once). Don't worry about this unless you hit a limit.
Model
The specific version of an AI. Claude Sonnet, GPT-4, etc. Different models have different strengths. Newer = generally smarter, but also more expensive.
Fine-tuning
Training an AI on your specific data so it learns your style, voice, or domain. Advanced — you don't need this to start, but it's how brands get truly custom AI.
Automation
Making tasks happen automatically without you having to do them. Buffer scheduling your posts = automation. ARIA managing your content calendar = automation.
Workflow
The sequence of steps you follow to get something done. Your "1 topic → 12 posts → schedule → publish" process is a workflow.
Orchestration
Managing multiple AI agents and making them work together. That's exactly what you're doing — you're the orchestra conductor, your agents are the musicians.
Midjourney
An AI tool that generates images from text descriptions. You describe what you want, it creates a professional image. Used for your avatar and visual content.
Content Calendar
A schedule that maps out what you're posting, where, and when. ARIA manages this for you. It keeps you consistent without having to think "what do I post today?"
UGC (User Generated Content)
Casual, authentic-looking content — like a video you film on your phone. UGC converts better than polished production because it feels real and relatable.
Hook
The first line, sentence, or visual of your content that makes someone stop scrolling. The most important 3 seconds of any piece of content you create.
CTA (Call To Action)
The instruction you give your audience at the end of content — "Follow for more," "Click the link in bio," "Comment YES if this resonates." Every post needs one.
A/B Testing
Running two versions of something (two ad headlines, two hooks, two thumbnails) to see which one performs better. Then doing more of the winner.
Brand Voice
The consistent personality and tone of your content. If your brand voice is "bold, funny, and direct," every piece of content should sound bold, funny, and direct — whoever (or whatever AI) wrote it.
Engagement Rate
The percentage of people who interact with your content (likes, comments, shares, saves) compared to those who saw it. Higher = better. 3-5% is good. 10%+ is great.

Protect Yourself

Legal Stuff You Need To Know Before You Post

This isn't meant to scare you — it's meant to protect you. 5 minutes of reading now saves headaches later.

The Quick Facts

FTC DISCLOSURE

You must disclose when content is AI-generated, sponsored, or involves a paid partnership. Use #ad, #sponsored, or #AIGenerated in your captions. The FTC is actively enforcing this — don't skip it.

COPYRIGHT

AI-generated images are not automatically copyrightable under current US law. Use original prompts, document your creative process, and never copy another creator's work. For music in videos — only use royalty-free tracks (Epidemic Sound, Artlist, or YouTube Audio Library).

PLATFORM TERMS OF SERVICE

TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Twitter/X all allow AI-generated content — but require disclosure. Read each platform's current AI content policy. They change frequently. GUARDIAN should flag any content that might violate platform rules before it goes live.

LIKENESS & PRIVACY

Don't create AI avatars that look like real people (celebrities, public figures, or private individuals) without their consent. Don't include other people's personal data in your AI prompts. Your AI avatar must be original to you.

BUSINESS & TAX

If you're making money, you need a business entity. An LLC (Limited Liability Company) is the standard starting point — it protects your personal assets and makes taxes cleaner. Register at your state's Secretary of State website (most states cost $50–$150). Consult a CPA for tax setup.
